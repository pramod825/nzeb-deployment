ℹ️ About This Project

Understanding Net-Zero Energy Buildings and how this system works

What is a Net-Zero Energy Building?

🏢 What is NZEB?

A Net-Zero Energy Building (NZEB) is a building that produces as much energy as it consumes over a year. The energy is typically generated through renewable sources like solar panels integrated into the building structure (BIPV).

In India, with abundant solar radiation (4–7 kWh/m²/day), NZEBs are achievable across most climate zones through smart design, energy-efficient systems, and Building-Integrated Photovoltaics.

☀️ What is BIPV?

Building-Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV) are solar panels embedded directly into building elements — rooftops, facades, windows, and walls — replacing conventional building materials while generating clean electricity.

This system models BIPV on South, East, and West facades plus rooftop coverage of 700m² (70% of 1000m² roof area) using 18% efficient panels with a 0.8 performance ratio.

🎯 How to Achieve Net Zero in India

1

Reduce Demand

High-performance envelope, low-U glazing, shading devices, and efficient HVAC systems

2

Generate Solar

Install BIPV on rooftop and South/West facades to maximize solar energy generation

3

Store Energy

Battery storage systems to use solar energy during non-daylight hours

4

Monitor & Optimize

Real-time monitoring and ML-based optimization to maintain Net Zero status
